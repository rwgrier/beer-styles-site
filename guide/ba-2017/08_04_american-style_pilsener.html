American-Style Pilsener

  • Color: Straw to gold
  • Clarity: Chill haze should not be present
  • Perceived Malt Aroma & Flavor: Medium-low to medium
  • Perceived Hop Aroma & Flavor: Hop aroma and ï¬‚avor is medium to high, traditionally derived from noble-type hops, but aromas can also be from various American hop varieties.
  • Perceived bitterness: Medium to high
  • Fermentation Characteristics: DMS, fruity-estery and diacetyl aromas and flavors should be absent.
  • Body: Medium-low to medium
  • Additional notes: Up to 25% corn and/or rice in the grist should be used. This style represents the classic and unique pre-Prohibition American-Style Pilsener. When using these guidelines as the basis for evaluating entries at competitions, organizers may wish to subcategorize this category into rice and corn subcategories.

Vital Statistics:

  • OG: 1.045 - 1.060
  • FG: 1.012 - 1.018
  • IBUs: 25 - 40
  • SRM: 3 - 6
  • ABV: 4.9 - 6.0%
